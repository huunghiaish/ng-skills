# E-E-A-T Evaluation Framework
## Updated per Google QRG Sept 2025 + Dec 2025 Core Update

E-E-A-T = **E**xperience, **E**xpertise, **A**uthoritativeness, **T**rustworthiness.
Trustworthiness is most important, assessed via the other three + direct trust signals.

## Dec 2025 Core Update
- E-E-A-T now applies to ALL competitive queries, not just YMYL
- Author attribution standards tighter across all categories
- AI content quality detection significantly improved
- Even lifestyle/entertainment content requires demonstrated expertise

## YMYL Topics (highest E-E-A-T required)
Health/safety, financial advice, legal info, news, elections, democratic processes, groups of people.

---

## Experience (Weight: 20%)
First-hand knowledge and personal involvement.
- [ ] Author has demonstrable first-hand experience
- [ ] Original photos, screenshots, or data
- [ ] Case studies with specific details, before/after results
- [ ] Process documentation, personal methodology
- [ ] Specific anecdotes that couldn't be fabricated

**Scoring:** Strong (multiple signals) → Moderate (some) → Weak (generic) → None (AI/scraped)

## Expertise (Weight: 25%)
Formal qualifications and demonstrated knowledge.
- [ ] Author credentials relevant to topic (bio, certifications)
- [ ] Technical accuracy and depth appropriate for audience
- [ ] Claims supported by evidence/sources
- [ ] Up-to-date with current developments
- [ ] Byline with author name and credentials visible

**Scoring:** Strong (verified credentials) → Moderate (some knowledge) → Weak (surface-level) → None (errors)

## Authoritativeness (Weight: 25%)
Recognition by others as go-to source.
- [ ] Site/author recognized authority in niche
- [ ] Content cited by authoritative sources
- [ ] Industry awards, certifications, accreditations
- [ ] Consistent publication history in topic area
- [ ] Featured in reputable media

**Scoring:** Strong (widely recognized) → Moderate (growing) → Weak (none) → None (negative reputation)

## Trustworthiness (Weight: 30%)
Overall reliability and transparency.
- [ ] Clear contact info (address, phone, email)
- [ ] Privacy policy and terms of service
- [ ] HTTPS with valid certificate
- [ ] Transparent about content creators and purpose
- [ ] Customer reviews, testimonials
- [ ] Corrections and update history visible
- [ ] Secure payment (e-commerce)

**Scoring:** Strong (full transparency) → Moderate (minor gaps) → Weak (missing key signals) → None (deceptive)

---

## AI Content Assessment (Sept 2025 QRG)
- AI content acceptable if demonstrates genuine E-E-A-T with unique value
- Low-quality AI: generic phrasing, no original insight, repetitive, no author attribution
- Presence of AI is not inherently penalizing — unique value matters

## Overall Scoring
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 90-100 | Exceptional — authority site, recognized expert, full transparency |
| 70-89 | Strong — demonstrated expertise, good trust signals |
| 50-69 | Moderate — some signals, room for improvement |
| 30-49 | Weak — minimal signals, significant gaps |
| 0-29 | Very low — no visible signals, potential trust issues |

## Improvement by Score
- **0-29:** Add contact info, establish author identity, implement HTTPS
- **30-49:** Add author bios, include first-hand experience, get citations
- **50-69:** Deepen with original research, build content clusters
- **70-89:** Maintain freshness, expand author presence, pursue publications
- **90+:** Continue quality, monitor reputation, keep credentials current
