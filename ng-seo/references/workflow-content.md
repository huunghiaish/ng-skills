# Content Quality & E-E-A-T Analysis

Read `eeat-framework.md` for full criteria + scoring.

## E-E-A-T Quick Reference
- **Experience (20%):** first-hand signals, original research, case studies, photos
- **Expertise (25%):** credentials, technical depth, accurate claims
- **Authoritativeness (25%):** external citations, brand mentions, recognition
- **Trustworthiness (30%):** contact info, HTTPS, privacy policy, reviews, dates

## Content Metrics
- Word count vs page type minimums (see `quality-gates.md`)
- Readability: Flesch 60-70, sentence avg 15-20 words, paragraph 2-4 sentences
- Keyword: primary in title/H1/first 100 words, density 1-3%, semantic variations
- Structure: logical headings, scannable, ToC for long-form, bullet lists
- Internal links: 3-5 per 1,000 words, descriptive anchors

## AI Content Assessment
- Acceptable if demonstrates genuine E-E-A-T with unique value
- Low-quality markers: generic phrasing, no insight, repetitive, no attribution

## AI Citation Readiness (GEO)
- Quotable statements with stats, structured data, answer-first formatting
- Tables/lists for comparisons, clear attribution, topical authority

## Content Freshness
- Publication date visible, last updated if revised
- Flag >12 months without update for fast-changing topics
