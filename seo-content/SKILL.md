# Content Quality & E-E-A-T Analysis

## E-E-A-T Framework

Read `seo/references/eeat-framework.md` for full criteria + scoring.

### Experience — first-hand signals
- Original research, case studies, before/after results
- Personal anecdotes, process documentation, unique data
- Photos/videos from direct experience

### Expertise — qualifications
- Author credentials, certifications, bio relevant to topic
- Technical depth appropriate for audience, accurate claims

### Authoritativeness — recognition
- External citations, backlinks, brand mentions, industry recognition
- Consistent publication history in topic area

### Trustworthiness — reliability (most important)
- Contact info, privacy policy, HTTPS, date stamps
- Customer reviews, transparent corrections

## Content Metrics

### Word Count (see `seo/references/quality-gates.md`)
| Page Type | Minimum |
|-----------|---------|
| Homepage | 500 | Service | 800 | Blog | 1,500 |
| Product | 300+ | Location | 500-600 |

> Word count = topical coverage floor, NOT ranking factor.

### Readability
- Flesch Reading Ease: target 60-70 (quality indicator, not SEO metric)
- Sentence length: avg 15-20 words
- Paragraph length: 2-4 sentences

### Keyword Optimization
- Primary keyword in title, H1, first 100 words
- Natural density (1-3%), semantic variations present

### Content Structure
- Logical heading hierarchy (H1 → H2 → H3)
- Scannable sections, bullet/numbered lists
- ToC for long-form content (>1,500 words)

### Internal Linking
- 3-5 relevant internal links per 1,000 words
- Descriptive anchor text, no orphan pages

## AI Content Assessment

### Acceptable AI Content
- Demonstrates genuine E-E-A-T, unique value, human oversight

### Low-Quality AI Markers
- Generic phrasing, no original insight, repetitive structure
- No author attribution, factual inaccuracies

## AI Citation Readiness (GEO)

Optimize for AI search (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews):
- Clear, quotable statements with statistics/facts
- Structured data, strong heading hierarchy, answer-first formatting
- Tables/lists for comparative data, clear attribution
- First-party data, topical authority, entity clarity via schema

## Content Freshness
- Publication date visible, last updated date if revised
- Flag content >12 months without update for fast-changing topics
